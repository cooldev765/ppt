PowerDonor

The Problem & Solution

The Problem

Americans donate over $500 billion a year, but picking where to give is overwhelming. There are 1.8 million nonprofits in the US — how do you find the ones that actually match what you care about?

The Product

Meet Dafney — our AI interviewer. She has a natural conversation with the donor about what matters to them: the causes they care about, the places they love, the communities they want to help, and how involved they want to be. Then she finds the nonprofits that truly fit.

How It Works

  1. Conversation — Dafney talks with the donor for a few minutes
  2. Understanding — the system learns who the donor is, in their own words
  3. Philanthropy Policy Statement — a paragraph that reads like the donor's own mission statement
  4. Matches — 3–20 handpicked nonprofits from our database, each with a clear explanation of why it fits

Why It's Different

  • The donor's own words matter. If they say "dolphins near Bermuda," we find charities that actually work there.
  • No hidden agenda. We have no relationships with any nonprofit. We're fully donor-first.
  • Transparent. Every match explains why it matched and what didn't.
  • On the donor's terms. They pick the budget, involvement level, and causes.

What's in the Database

A single PostgreSQL database holds everything: every donor who uses the platform and every nonprofit we can recommend.

Three Core Parts

  • The Donor Table — Every person who talks to Dafney gets a row here. Stores their conversation, the system's understanding of them, the philanthropy statement, and match results.
  • The Charity Table (the heart) — 220,000+ US nonprofits with at least $100K in annual expenses and a website, each with deep profiles including who they are, what they do, who they serve, where they work, how to give, and their cause area.
  • The Financial Table — IRS tax filings for every charity — revenue, expenses, assets, and compensation data. Gives donors transparency and trust signals.

The Charity Data

Every charity in our database has a rich profile:

  • Who they are — Name, location, website, contact info, leadership
  • What they do — Mission, programs, impact metrics, staff count, year founded
  • Who they serve — 44 population groups (children, seniors, veterans, women & girls, people with disabilities, immigrants, and more)
  • Where they work — Local, statewide, national, or global reach
  • How you can engage — Donation options, volunteer opportunities, events
  • Their cause area — 11 categories: Arts, Basic Needs, Economic Empowerment, Education, Environment & Animals, Equity & Justice, Faith, International Aid, Health, Policy, Science & Tech

How We Built It

Started with 1.8M IRS records → found websites → crawled content → used AI to extract mission, programs, and impact → classified and indexed every charity with AI-powered semantic search fingerprints for real-time matching.

From Conversation to Profile

How Dafney's conversation becomes matching-ready data

What Dafney Captures

  • Location and opening narrative ("I care about...")
  • Cause areas, geography, and populations they want to help
  • Engagement style (give, volunteer, attend, advise)
  • Exemplar organizations and things to avoid
  • Specific passions, niche interests, and budget

The Compiled Profile

Five clear parts:

  • Identity — who they are
  • Capture — every word preserved
  • Compiled Profile — structured intent
  • Artifacts — philanthropy policy statement
  • Workflow State — experience progress

Why Both Matter

Structured preferences ("youth", "statewide", "K-12") + exact phrases ("foster youth aging out", "dolphins near Bermuda").

The result: Living profile powering statements, matching, and explanations.

The Matching Process

Dafney interviews the donor about causes, geography, population preferences, engagement style, and specific passions. The backend runs a 4-stage matching pipeline:

Detailed Stages

  • Stage 1 — Hard Filter: Narrow 220,000 charities to those in donor's chosen causes and must-have requirements
  • Stage 2 — Semantic Search: Use AI embeddings to find charities whose missions match donor's language, including niche preferences
  • Stage 3 — Smart Reranking: Balance relevance with variety for a meaningful mix
  • Stage 4 — AI Explanations: Each recommendation gets a plain-English explanation reflecting donor's values

Why This Matters

Example: "I care about foster youth aging out in North Carolina, especially housing stability and job readiness."

Without PowerDonor

Generic search returns 50+ broad education or youth charities. The donor manually sorts through them, wasting time and missing meaningful opportunities.

With PowerDonor

  • Smart Filtering: Filter to charities serving young adults (18-24) in North Carolina
  • Precise Matching: Match "foster youth aging out" as a named community
  • Focus Areas: Surface organizations focused on housing, job readiness, outcomes
  • Clear Explanations: Each match explains why it fits — and what doesn't

The Result

3–15 handpicked nonprofits the donor can meaningfully consider (based on what they want to see), instead of hundreds to sift through.
